Beaked Yucca >

Beaked yucca are hardy in both cold and hot environments. Their edible fruit offers some support for animals as well.

Spotted Spurge >

The Spotted Spurge is prolific in even the most challenging of situations. Its rapid seed production allows it to establish itself and hold its ground, even in difficult terrain like sidewalks and paths.

Ball Moss >

Ball Moss are often found dangling from trees, suspended acrobatically among the branches. They collect water from the air and make their own food.

Whale's Tongue Agave >

Whale's Tongue Agave is an adaptable bastion of a succulent. It is drought-resistant, immune to most diseases, and a shelter for insects.

Mexican Honeysuckle >

The Mexican Honeysuckle possesses orange blooms that tantalize the senses with their innate spicy fragrance.

Creeping Fig >

Creeping Fig grows rapidly and with purpose. When close to vertical surfaces, it climbs with dedication until it can no longer.

Southern Live Oak >

The expansive root structure of the Southern Live Oak ensures that no buildings encroach on its space. It has a repitoire of abilities, such as acorn production, inconspicuous flowering, and defoliation in cold winters.

'Soft Caress' Mahonia >

The 'Soft Caress' Mahonia is known for its appealing looks. It entrances the eye with its thin leaves and yellow buds.

Tricolor Oyster Plant >

The Tricolor Oyster Plant may look showy, but when outdoors, it grows best in the shade.

Japanese Loquat >

The Japanese Loquat is a flowering tree that blooms late into the year. Its fruit is attractive and nutritious to wildlife, and its flowers emit an aroma that is alluring to hummingbirds and butterflies.
